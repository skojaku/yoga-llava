\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\noindent\textbf{Human pose estimation and body reconstruction.}
Recovering the configuration of a human body from a single image is a mature problem.
In two dimensions, pose estimators already localize keypoints with high accuracy.
Early architectures reach this accuracy by fusing multi-scale features~\cite{newell2016stacked,xiao2018simple} and associating keypoints across people~\cite{cao2019openpose}.
Later designs preserve high-resolution features~\cite{sun2019deep} or replace convolutions with transformer backbones~\cite{xu2022vitpose}.
Beyond 2D joints, parametric models recover 3D pose and shape.
SMPL defines the standard representation~\cite{loper2015smpl}.
Around this representation, regressors estimate pose and shape from one image with increasing robustness~\cite{kanazawa2018end,kolotouros2019spin,li2022cliff,goel2023humans}.
SAM 3D Body extends this line to full-body recovery under the MHR representation~\cite{yang2026sam3dbody}.
In all of these methods, geometry is the final product.
We instead use reconstruction as an annotation mechanism, converting dense outputs into interpretable measurements that ground language supervision.

\noindent\textbf{Action quality and pose correction.}
Action quality assessment scores how well an action is performed~\cite{lei2019survey}, with methods for cross-action scoring~\cite{parmar2019action} and the assessment of rehabilitation exercises~\cite{liao2020deep}.
Within yoga and related exercise domains, Pose Tutor goes further and identifies the joint angles that drive a pose prediction~\cite{dittakavi2022posetutor}.
Such outputs say whether a pose is wrong without saying how the body is configured.
Our focus is complementary to this line of work: the language supervision we construct keeps every numeric statement traceable to an explicit, image-specific measurement.

\noindent\textbf{Vision--language learning and visual QA.}
Vision--language models connect images with text at scale.
CLIP establishes the recipe with contrastive pretraining over image--text pairs~\cite{radford2021clip}.
Building on such representations, Flamingo and BLIP-2 couple frozen image encoders with language models~\cite{alayrac2022flamingo,li2023blip2}.
Visual instruction tuning then teaches the coupled models to follow instructions~\cite{liu2023visual,dai2023instructblip}, with Qwen2-VL adding dynamic-resolution processing~\cite{wang2024qwen2vl}.
The benchmarks behind this progress remain object-centric.
VQAv2 and GQA ask about objects, attributes, and relational reasoning~\cite{goyal2017vqav2,hudson2019gqa}.
Neither asks the model to select a continuous body measurement and verbalize it.
YogaAlign supplies this missing supervision.

\noindent\textbf{Human pose datasets.}
Existing pose datasets do not close this gap.
COCO and MPII annotate 2D keypoints at scale~\cite{lin2014coco,andriluka2014mpii}.
For 3D, Human3.6M provides motion capture under controlled conditions~\cite{ionescu2014human36m}.
3DPW extends 3D annotation to in-the-wild video~\cite{vonMarcard2018}.
Closest to our setting, Yoga-82 provides a large and visually diverse benchmark of 82 asanas~\cite{verma2020yoga82}, yet its supervision stops at hierarchical category labels.
No existing dataset pairs an image with measured body geometry and language grounded in that geometry.
YogaAlign builds on the image diversity of Yoga-82 and adds reconstruction-derived measurements with language-grounded supervision.
