\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\noindent\textbf{Human pose estimation and body reconstruction.}
Recovering the configuration of a human body from a single image is a mature problem.
Two-dimensional pose estimators localize keypoints with high accuracy.
Early architectures fuse multi-scale features~\cite{newell2016stacked,xiao2018simple} and associate keypoints across people~\cite{cao2019openpose}.
Later designs preserve high-resolution features~\cite{sun2019deep} or adopt transformer backbones~\cite{xu2022vitpose}.
Parametric models extend recovery from 2D joints to 3D pose and shape.
SMPL defines the standard representation~\cite{loper2015smpl}.
Regressors estimate its parameters from one image with increasing robustness~\cite{kanazawa2018end,kolotouros2019spin,li2022cliff,goel2023humans}.
SAM 3D Body extends this line to full-body recovery under the MHR representation~\cite{yang2026sam3dbody}.
These methods output geometry as the final product.
We use reconstruction as an annotation mechanism instead.
Dense outputs are converted into interpretable measurements that ground language supervision.

\noindent\textbf{Action quality and pose correction.}
Action quality assessment scores how well an action is performed~\cite{lei2019survey}.
Methods exist for cross-action quality scoring~\cite{parmar2019action} and for assessment of rehabilitation exercises~\cite{liao2020deep}.
Pose Tutor identifies the joint angles that drive a pose prediction in yoga and related exercise domains~\cite{dittakavi2022posetutor}.
These approaches return scores, error indicators, or localized corrections.
They do not verbalize the measured configuration of the body.
Our focus is complementary.
We construct language supervision whose numeric statements remain traceable to explicit, image-specific measurements.

\noindent\textbf{Vision--language learning and visual QA.}
Vision--language models connect images with text at scale.
CLIP learns transferable image--text representations through contrastive pretraining~\cite{radford2021clip}.
Flamingo and BLIP-2 couple frozen image encoders with language models~\cite{alayrac2022flamingo,li2023blip2}.
Visual instruction tuning then teaches these models to follow instructions~\cite{liu2023visual,dai2023instructblip}, with Qwen2-VL adding dynamic-resolution processing~\cite{wang2024qwen2vl}.
Standard VQA benchmarks ask about objects, attributes, and relations~\cite{goyal2017vqav2,hudson2019gqa}.
They do not ask a model to select a continuous body measurement and verbalize it.
YogaAlign supplies this missing supervision.

\noindent\textbf{Human pose datasets.}
Existing pose datasets do not close this gap.
COCO and MPII annotate 2D keypoints~\cite{lin2014coco,andriluka2014mpii}.
Human3.6M captures 3D pose under controlled conditions~\cite{ionescu2014human36m}.
3DPW extends 3D annotation to in-the-wild video~\cite{vonMarcard2018}.
Yoga-82 targets fine-grained classification of 82 asanas but provides only category labels~\cite{verma2020yoga82}.
No dataset pairs an image with measured body geometry and language grounded in that geometry.
YogaAlign builds on the image diversity of Yoga-82 and adds reconstruction-derived measurements with language-grounded supervision.
